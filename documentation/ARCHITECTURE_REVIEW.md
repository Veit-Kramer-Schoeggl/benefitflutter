## 1. Ehrliches Gesamturteil

Die Architektur ist **überdurchschnittlich gut für ein Bachelorprojekt — und KEIN Rewrite-Kandidat.**
Die Fundamente stimmen:

- `provider` + `ChangeNotifier` mit **Single-Source-of-Truth** (`AuthProvider` besitzt `userId`,
  `ChangeNotifierProxyProvider` verteilt sie an abhängige Provider, die bei Logout zurücksetzen;
  ✅ seit Phase 1 / Round 2 ist der frühere `UserProvider` in `AuthProvider` + `ProfileProvider` aufgeteilt),
- ein sauberer **azyklischer feature-first-Abhängigkeitsgraph**,
- **Repository-Interface/Impl-Nähte**,
- eine durchdachte **Strategy-pro-Entity-Sync-Abstraktion** (`BaseSyncStrategy<T>`),
- gut gefasste Domain-Config-Klassen und ein zentraler SQLite-Type-Converter,
- eine substanzielle Unit-Suite (~1.374 Assertions).

**Aber: nicht produktions- bzw. skalierungsreif.** Das Problem ist nicht die Architekturwahl,
sondern dass die produktionskritischen Schichten entweder **gar nicht realisiert** (echtes
Backend/Sync, Auth, Observability, i18n, Background-Tracking) oder mit **konkreten Daten-/
Sicherheits-Defekten** behaftet sind.

Der höchste ROI liegt in einer kurzen **„Stabilisieren-und-Instrumentieren"-Phase** (Tage, nicht
Wochen), die jeden späteren Schritt absichert — **nicht** in Framework-Migrationen.

## 3. Roadmap in Phasen

Sequenziert nach Risiko & Abhängigkeit. Aufwand: **S** < 1 Tag · **M** 1–3 Tage · **L** ~1 Woche · **XL** > 1 Woche.

### Phase 0 — Stabilisieren & instrumentieren *(Tage)*
Ziel: Blocker entfernen, Code sicher änderbar machen, Produktions-Sichtbarkeit gewinnen — **bevor**
ein riskantes Refactoring beginnt.

- Alle **Sofort-Blocker (§2)** ausliefern.
- **Globaler Error-Handler** in `main.dart` (`FlutterError.onError` + `PlatformDispatcher.onError`
  + `runZonedGuarded`). **(S)** — Voraussetzung für Crash-Reporting.
- **Crash-Reporting** (`sentry_flutter`, DSGVO-freundlicher als Firebase) mit `beforeSend`-PII-Scrubbing. **(M)**
- **CI (GitHub Actions):** `format --set-exit-if-changed`, `analyze --fatal-infos`, `test --coverage`,
  Debug-Build — als required check. **(M)** → Voraussetzung für jedes spätere Refactoring.
- **Analyzer härten** (`strict-casts`/`strict-raw-types`, `avoid_print`, Import-Boundary-Lints). **(M)**
- **Schlanke `AppLogger`-Fassade** (`logger`) mit Leveln + Redaction; Error-Level an Sentry. **(M)**

### Phase 1 — Korrektheit, Datenintegrität & Entkopplung *(vor Skalierung)*
**Reihenfolge kritisch:** erst *eine* Schema-Quelle → *dann* Migrationstests → *dann* Orphan-Cleanup
→ *dann* FK aktivieren (sonst zementieren Tests die heutige `onCreate`-vs-`onUpgrade`-Divergenz).

- **Schema-Single-Source-of-Truth:** doppelte v11-Definition / `onCreate`-ruft-`_migrateToV4` auflösen. **(S)**
- **Migrationstests** gegen In-Memory-SQLite (`sqflite_common_ffi`); in CI. **(M)**
- **`db.transaction`** um Mehrzeilen-Writes (awardBenefit, Session-Finalize+Segments+Summary) +
  **`UNIQUE(users.email)`** nach De-Dup. **(M)**
- **DI-Naht in jeden Provider** → kaputte Provider-Tests reparieren (heute 7 grün / 23 rot),
  Tests für `AuthProvider`/`ProfileProvider`/`ProgressProvider`/`AppLockProvider`. **(M)**
- ✅ **Erledigt (Phase 1 / Round 2):** **`UserProvider` aufteilen** in `AuthProvider` (Tokens/Login/Refresh/Rate-Limit, besitzt `userId`)
  + `ProfileProvider` (User-CRUD); transiente `_pending*`-Felder in screen-scoped State.
  *Auf provider bleiben — kein Framework-Wechsel.* **(L)** → Umgesetzt: `UserProvider` ist gelöscht,
  `AuthProvider` ist die einzige Identitäts-Quelle, `ProfileProvider` besitzt die editierbaren Profildaten.
- **Typisierte `AppConfig`** via `--dart-define-from-file` (dev/staging/prod). **(M)** → Basis für Cert-Pins, Backend, Flavors.
- **`go_router`** mit Redirect-basiertem Auth-Guard (`StatefulShellRoute` für die 5 Tabs) — *kein* `auto_route`. **(L)**
- **Widget-Test-Layer** für kritische Flows mit dem vorhandenen `pump_app.dart`. **(L)**

### Phase 2 — Echtes Backend, Sync & Produktions-Auth *(der große Schritt)*

- **Background-Tracking-Runtime:** Android Foreground-Service (`FOREGROUND_SERVICE_LOCATION` +
  Notification), iOS `UIBackgroundModes:location`. Ohne das nimmt „tägliche Bewegung belohnen" im
  Produktivbetrieb **nichts** auf. **(L)**
- **Sync funktionsfähig machen:** Heute hat `sync_queue` keinen DAO, `processQueue()` keine Aufrufer
  → Offline-Writes gehen verloren. `SyncManager` + `SyncQueueDao` (Drain bei Reconnect, Backoff+Jitter,
  Dead-Letter) **oder** Sync-Engine kaufen. **(L)**
- **Backend-Entscheidung als deferred Spike (nicht P0):** PowerSync/Supabase vs. hand-gebautes
  PostgREST seriös evaluieren — `BaseSyncStrategy` bleibt die Tausch-Naht. → siehe geplantes
  Decision-Record (Schritt 3 nach dieser Phase).
- **Versionierte, idempotente Konfliktauflösung** statt blind Last-Write-Wins; **Benefits als
  append-only Ledger** — eine geldwerte App darf nicht doppelt/lückenhaft gutschreiben. **(M)**
- **Echte Auth:** `RealAuthService` + `ApiClient` + `AuthInterceptor` + echte SPKI-Cert-Pins;
  **Passwort-Hashing server-seitig (Argon2id+Salt+Pepper)** — unsalted Single-Round-SHA-256 ist die
  falsche Schicht; Refresh-Token-Rotation/Revocation. **(L)**
- **Sync-Observability + Remote-Kill-Switch** bevor der Netzwerkpfad live geht. **(M)**

### Phase 3 — Modernisierung, Scale-Polish & Rollout-Reife

- **i18n gesplittet:** *jetzt* nur das billige Gerüst (`flutter_localizations` + `l10n.yaml` + leeres
  `app_en.arb`/`app_de.arb`, **S**); die XL-Extraktion der ~189 Strings **erst wenn Launch-Locale feststeht**.
- **Build-Flavors (dev/staging/prod)** + CD (fastlane/Actions) für signierte Builds. **(L)**
- **Theming als Single-Source** (Token-Layer, `darkTheme`/`themeMode`; 324 hardcodierte Farben
  migrieren) + **Accessibility-Baseline** (`Semantics`, Text-Scaling, Kontrast). **(L)**
- **GPS-Retention** (`deleteOlderThan` + VACUUM, gated auf synced) + Douglas-Peucker-Polyline-
  Vereinfachung auf der Routenkarte. **(M)**
- **Feature-Konsolidierung** (presentation/providers in `features/<x>/`) — **P2/P3**, „boundary by
  convention für neuen Code" via Lints; großer File-Move **nicht** parallel zur Award-Arbeit. **(L)**

## 4. Übergreifende Lücken (kein Lens besaß sie — kritisch für eine DE-Health-App)

Teils so harte Launch-Blocker wie die Release-Signierung; je eine:n Owner geben.

- **DSGVO / Art. 9 (besondere Kategorien):** Rechtsgrundlage + Consent-Flow für kontinuierliches GPS +
  Herzfrequenz, Datenexport (Portabilität), **server-seitige Löschpropagierung** (Account-Löschung
  existiert nur lokal), Datenschutzerklärung, Permission-Rationale-UX.
- **Store-Policy Background-Location:** Google Play verlangt separate Deklaration + Review + prominente
  In-App-Disclosure; iOS „Always"-Rationale. Der Foreground-Service kann perfekt sein und trotzdem
  abgelehnt werden.
- **Datenverlust im Offline-only-Zeitraum:** alle erspielten Rewards in *einer* lokalen SQLite-DB,
  kein Export/Backup, `allowBackup` unspezifiziert → Handyverlust/Reinstall löscht alles.
- **Reward-Integrity / Anti-Cheat:** Validierung komplett client-seitig + client-generierte UUIDs →
  gerooteter/repackter Client kann Sessions fälschen und unbegrenzt Rewards minten.
- **Akku-Budget:** kein mAh/h-Ziel, keine adaptive Sampling-Rate, kein Doze/App-Standby-Test.
- **Rollout-Mechanik:** Staged/Percentage-Rollout, **Force-Update / Min-Version-Gate**,
  API-Contract-Versionierung für eine Multi-Version-Client-Basis.

## 5. Bewusst NICHT tun (Anti-Over-Engineering)

- ❌ **Kein** Wechsel zu **Riverpod/Bloc** — SSoT+ProxyProvider ist korrekt; die echten Probleme
  (God-Object, Testbarkeit, Rebuild-Scope) sind *innerhalb* von provider lösbar.

  nur die `RepositoryConfig`-Getter typisieren (geben heute `dynamic` zurück).

  XL-Codegen-Migration. (drift höchstens später, bewusst, als Pilot.)
- ❌ **Kein** `mockito`/`mocktail` + build_runner — handgeschriebene Mocks reichen; die Lücke ist
  Breite (Widget/Integration-Tests), nicht der Mocking-Stil.
- ❌ **Kein** melos-Monorepo — geteiltes `database_helper`-Schema ist ein harter Blocker;
  Import-Boundary-Lints geben ~90 % des Nutzens für ~10 % der Kosten.
- ❌ **Kein** `auto_route`, **kein** `fl_chart`/syncfusion (winzige fixe Datensätze),
  **keine** Runtime-Config aus den Domain-Config-Klassen.

## Anhang A — Befunde je Dimension

_Verdichtet aus den 10 unabhängigen Experten-Bewertungen. Aufwand S/M/L/XL, Priorität P0–P3._


### State Management & Dependency Injection

> ✅ **Update (Phase 1 / Round 2):** Die zentrale Empfehlung dieses Abschnitts — `UserProvider`
> aufteilen — ist **umgesetzt**. `UserProvider` wurde in `AuthProvider` (Identität/Session/Tokens/Auth-
> Flows, besitzt `userId`) + `ProfileProvider` (editierbare Profildaten: `updateUser`, Biometrics,
> Preferences) aufgeteilt. `AuthProvider` ist als erstes im `MultiProvider` registriert; `ProgressProvider`,
> `ActivityProvider` und `BenefitProvider` hängen jetzt als `ChangeNotifierProxyProvider<AuthProvider, X>`
> an `AuthProvider.userId`. Der nachfolgende **Ist-Zustand / Bewertung / Risiken**-Text beschreibt
> bewusst den **am Code verifizierten Audit-Stand vor dem Split** (Begründung der Maßnahme).

**Ist-Zustand:** State is managed with `provider` + `ChangeNotifier`. `lib/main.dart` (lines 64-124) wires a `MultiProvider` with `UserProvider` registered first, then `BenefitProvider`, `ProgressProvider`, and `ActivityProvider` as `ChangeNotifierProxyProvider<UserProvider, X>` whose `update` callback pushes `userProvider.userId` into each child via `updateUserId(...)`; `ConnectivityProvider`, `HealthPlatformProvider`, and `AppLockProvider` are plain `ChangeNotifierProvider`s. There are 8 ChangeNotifier providers total (12 ChangeNotifier subclasses exist in lib/, the rest are in markdown plan files). DI is manual: dependencies are passed via constructors, and concrete implementations come from a hand-written static factory `RepositoryConfig` (lib/core/config/repository_config.dart) whose three getters return `dynamic` (type erasure at the boundary). There is no service locator (no get_it/injectable) and no codegen. UserProvider (lib/providers/user_provider.dart, 894 lines, 62 notifyListeners calls) is the dominant object, mixing authentication, token/session lifecycle, user CRUD, registration+email-verification, password reset, account deletion, change-password, rate limiting, and a half-dozen 'pending*' transient form fields. activity_provider.dart (868 lines) similarly fuses tracking state machine + GPS stream + BLE heart-rate stream + DB persistence + timer. UI consumption is coarse: 31 `Consumer<T>`, 67 `context.read`, 5 `context.watch`, and ZERO `Selector`/`context.select` — so every notifyListeners rebuilds the whole Consumer subtree. Cross-provider data flow (userId) is unidirectional via ProxyProvider; providers that hold a non-null userId reload data on change and clear state on logout.


**Bewertung:** The architecture is genuinely sound in its fundamentals and is NOT a candidate for a framework rewrite. The single-source-of-truth pattern (UserProvider owns identity; ProxyProvider fans userId out to dependent providers; each provider resets on logout) is a correct, idiomatic provider solution to a real problem (the codebase previously had hardcoded 'test-user-123' scattered everywhere — see AUTH_PROVIDER_PLAN.md). Constructor DI is clean and explicit, repositories are interface-typed inside the providers, and DatabaseHelper is a proper singleton so the repo factories are cheap and share one connection. Several providers (BenefitProvider, ConnectivityProvider, AppLockProvider) are well-sized, well-documented, and have a clear single responsibility. That said, the implementation has three real weaknesses that bite specifically at the stated goal of large-scale, modular, modern. (1) UserProvider is a 894-line god-object that conflates four distinct concerns (auth lifecycle, user profile, multi-step account flows, rate limiting) plus ephemeral form state that has no business living in a long-lived app-scoped singleton. (2) Testability is actively broken, not just theoretical: ActivityProvider and HealthPlatformProvider hard-instantiate their collaborators in constructor defaults / field initializers (SensorManager, GpsPointDao, BleDataSource, two DAOs that reach the real DatabaseHelper; HealthSyncService()), and ActivityProvider's userId can ONLY be set through ProxyProvider — there is no injection seam — so 'flutter test test/unit/providers/' currently reports 7 passing / 23 failing, and the three highest-risk providers (UserProvider, ProgressProvider, AppLockProvider) have NO unit tests at all. (3) Rebuild scope is uniformly coarse: with zero Selector/select usage, a once-per-second timer tick in ActivityProvider rebuilds every Consumer<ActivityProvider> subtree, and the 62 notifyListeners in UserProvider rebuild all five Consumer<UserProvider> auth screens regardless of which field changed. None of these require Riverpod/Bloc to fix — they are fixable inside provider — but they are real and will compound at scale.


**Skalierungs-Risiken:**

- 🔴 **critical** — Testability is broken by construction. Providers hard-instantiate collaborators in constructor-default args / field initializers (ActivityProvider's SensorManager+DAOs, HealthPlatformProvider's HealthSyncService), and ActivityProvider has no seam to set userId outside ProxyProvider. Result today: provider unit suite is 7 passing / 23 failing, and UserProvider/ProgressProvider/AppLockProvider have zero tests. At scale this means regressions in the most critical (auth, tracking) code paths ship undetected.
- 🟠 **high** — UserProvider is a 894-line god-object holding auth + tokens + user profile + 7 transient 'pending*' fields + rate limiter, with 62 notifyListeners and 5 Consumer<UserProvider> subscribers. As auth/profile features grow it becomes a merge-conflict hotspot and a single point of failure; every auth screen rebuilds on any of its 62 notifications.
- 🟡 **medium** — No selective rebuilds (0 Selector / 0 context.select). ActivityProvider fires notifyListeners every second from its tracking timer, rebuilding the entire Consumer<ActivityProvider> subtree (map/charts/buttons) once per second during a workout. On low-end Android at public-rollout scale this is a measurable jank/battery cost.
- 🟡 **medium** — RepositoryConfig getters return `dynamic`, erasing types at the central composition root. A wrong repository wired into a provider is not caught at compile time, only at runtime — dangerous as the number of repositories and providers grows.
- ⚪ **low** — ChangeNotifierProxyProvider lifecycle footgun: ProgressProvider calls loadActivities() in its constructor AND on updateUserId, and side-effecting async work is triggered from update callbacks. As the provider graph grows, ordering/duplicate-load bugs and 'setState/notify during build' classes of issues become likely.


**Empfehlungen:**

- **[P0 · M · impact:critical] Add a DI/injection seam to every provider and fix the broken provider test suite** — Make all collaborators injectable instead of default-constructed inside the provider: ActivityProvider already accepts optional SensorManager/DAOs but tests don't pass fakes and there is no way to set userId in isolation — add an injectable identity seam (e.g. accept a userId provider/callback or expose @visibleForTesting updateUserId in tests) ; HealthPlatformProvider must take HealthSyncService via constructor (currently `final _syncService = HealthSyncService();`). Then write the missing unit tests for AuthProvider/ProfileProvider (the two providers the former UserProvider was split into), ProgressProvider, and AppLockProvider, and repair the 23 failing provider tests. _Warum:_ Test verification shows 7 passing / 23 failing in test/unit/providers, with the three highest-risk providers untested. For an app targeting public rollout, the auth and tracking state machines are exactly the code that must not regress. The fix is cheap because the constructors are nearly there already. _Modern:_ Constructor injection of interfaces (current pattern) is correct — just complete it. Optionally add mockito/mocktail for fakes. No framework change needed.
- **[P1 · L · impact:high] ✅ Done (Phase 1 / Round 2): Split UserProvider into AuthProvider + ProfileProvider (+ extract pending-flow state)** — *Shipped: `UserProvider` was deleted and split into `AuthProvider` (identity/sessions/tokens + all auth & account flows, single source of `userId`, exposing `setCurrentUser`/`refreshUser`) and `ProfileProvider` (`updateUser`, biometrics, preferences; writes the repository first, then syncs identity via `AuthProvider.setCurrentUser(...)`). `ProgressProvider`/`ActivityProvider`/`BenefitProvider` now proxy off `AuthProvider`.* Original recommendation, kept for context — Decompose the 894-line UserProvider along its seams: an AuthProvider owning tokens/login/logout/refresh/rate-limiting and exposing the reactive userId that ProxyProvider already depends on; a ProfileProvider owning currentUser CRUD/biometrics/preferences; and move the 7 transient _pending* fields (registration name/email/password/verification code, reset email/code, deletion email) out of the app-scoped singleton into per-flow controllers or screen-scoped state. Keep AuthProvider as the single source of userId so the existing ProxyProvider wiring in main.dart barely changes. _Warum:_ These four concerns have different lifetimes (app-long auth vs per-screen registration flow) and change for different reasons. Splitting cuts the merge-conflict surface, shrinks rebuild fan-out (auth-token change no longer rebuilds profile-only widgets), and makes each piece independently testable. A ProfileProvider stub is already referenced by 3 Consumers and 1 watch in the UI (lib/presentation/screens/profile) but the class does not exist in lib/providers — so part of this split is already assumed by the codebase. _Modern:_ Stay on provider; split into multiple ChangeNotifiers. Do NOT introduce Riverpod/Bloc just for this.
- **[P1 · S · impact:medium] Type the composition root: make RepositoryConfig return interfaces, not dynamic** — Change RepositoryConfig.getUserRepository/getSessionRepository/getBenefitRepository return types from `dynamic` to the repository interfaces (UserRepository, SessionRepository, BenefitRepository). No behavior change; pure type annotation. _Warum:_ The central wiring point currently erases types, so a mis-wired repository fails only at runtime. Typing it restores compile-time safety at the one place where it matters most, and is a 15-minute change. _Modern:_ Keep the static factory pattern (it is fine at this scale) — just type it. Do NOT pull in get_it/injectable yet: with ~3 repos and 8 providers the manual root is simpler and has zero magic.
- **[P2 · M · impact:medium] Introduce selective rebuilds where a provider notifies on a hot path** — Add Selector / context.select around the high-churn consumers: ActivityProvider's per-second timer should only rebuild the elapsed-time widget (Selector on elapsedSeconds / currentDistance / currentHeartRate), not the whole tracking screen; auth screens should select the specific fields (isLoading, error) rather than Consume the whole AuthProvider. Optionally split ActivityProvider's high-frequency tracking values into a small dedicated notifier. _Warum:_ Zero Selector/select usage today means every notifyListeners rebuilds the full Consumer subtree. ActivityProvider fires once per second during a workout; this is avoidable jank/battery on the low-end Android devices a public rollout will hit. _Modern:_ provider's built-in Selector/context.select. (If ActivityProvider's stream-heavy hot path keeps growing, that single provider — not the whole app — is the strongest future Riverpod candidate, because Riverpod's granular providers + autoDispose model this best.)
- **[P2 · S · impact:low] Move side-effecting async out of provider constructors / ProxyProvider update** — ProgressProvider triggers loadActivities() from its constructor and again from updateUserId; HealthPlatformProvider/ConnectivityProvider kick async _initialize() from constructors. Move first-load to an explicit initialize() called once (as AppLockProvider/AuthProvider already do via postFrameCallback / splash), and ensure updateUserId only reloads on actual change (it already guards) without duplicate loads. _Warum:_ Async work in constructors and in ProxyProvider update callbacks is the classic source of duplicate loads and 'notify during build' bugs as the graph grows. Making initialization explicit and idempotent is cheap insurance for scale. _Modern:_ Explicit initialize() pattern (already used by two providers) — keep it consistent across all. No new dependency.


**So lassen (nicht ändern):**

- ✅ provider + ChangeNotifier as the core state solution — it is appropriate for this app's scale and team, well understood, and a Riverpod/Bloc migration would cost weeks (28+ consumer sites, 8 providers) for benefits this codebase can mostly get by refactoring within provider. Do not migrate frameworks now.
- ✅ The single-source-of-truth + ProxyProvider(userId) fan-out pattern. It correctly solved the prior hardcoded-userId problem (AUTH_PROVIDER_PLAN.md), is idiomatic, and the unidirectional userId flow with per-provider reset-on-logout is exactly right. Keep the wiring shape; just split the providers behind it.
- ✅ Manual constructor DI over a service locator. With ~3 repositories and 8 providers, get_it/injectable would add ceremony and runtime indirection for no real gain. Manual DI is more explicit and debuggable here — revisit only if the provider/repo count multiplies.
- ✅ The static RepositoryConfig factory as the single composition root — the concept is good (one place to swap SQLite vs future remote impls). Only its `dynamic` return types need fixing, not the pattern.
- ✅ Smaller single-responsibility providers (BenefitProvider, ConnectivityProvider, AppLockProvider) — these are well-sized and documented; do not merge or over-abstract them.
- ✅ Keeping ActivityProvider as ONE provider despite its 868 lines is defensible because GPS/HR/timer/state-machine are genuinely one cohesive tracking lifecycle; prefer extracting a hot-path notifier for rebuild scope over splitting the domain logic prematurely.


### Data Layer, Persistence & Migrations

**Ist-Zustand:** Raw sqflite (sqflite, no version constraint in pubspec) behind a singleton DatabaseHelper (lib/features/shared/database/database_helper.dart, 773 lines) that owns ALL schema DDL, onCreate, and hand-written sequential onUpgrade migrations up to schema version 11. Per-entity persistence is hand-written DAOs (14 files under lib/features/*/data/*_dao.dart and .../daos/*_dao.dart) that issue db.query/insert/update/delete with string table names, string WHERE clauses, and positional args, plus manual Map<->model conversion via _fromMap/_toMap. Type marshalling is centralized in lib/features/shared/utils/sqlite_type_converters.dart (DateTime<->epoch ms, bool<->int, enum<->TEXT, lenient JSON DateTime parsing). Some models (e.g. SessionSensorSummary, SensorDataPoint in lib/features/wearable_integration/domain/sensor_data_point.dart) carry their own toJson/fromJson that double as the SQLite row map, with jsonEncode/jsonDecode for JSON-blob columns (heart_rate_zones, data_sources, metadata). Migrations defensively re-check schema with PRAGMA table_info before ALTER (v5-v9), and one data migration (v10) rehashes plaintext passwords row-by-row. Test coverage for the data layer is effectively zero: the only DB-related test is test/unit/shared/utils/sqlite_type_converters_test.dart (pure function tests); there are NO migration tests and NO DAO/integration tests against a real (in-memory) sqlite.


**Bewertung:** This is a competent, readable hand-rolled data layer that is appropriate for Phase 1 and genuinely better than a lot of student code: DAOs are thin and single-responsibility, queries are parameterized (no SQL injection), indexes are deliberately placed on hot columns (user_id, status, tracking_date, gps_points(session_id,timestamp)), batch inserts are used for the high-volume gps_points/biometric/motion paths, and the type-converter centralization is a real strength that keeps the epoch-ms convention consistent. **Ist-Zustand:** The app is genuinely offline-first today: writes go to SQLite first, then optionally sync (e.g. session_repository_impl.dart:123-141 saves locally, checks connectivity_service.isOnline(), tries uploadToRemote, falls back to queueForSync; user_repository_impl.dart:77-117 follows the same local-first pattern). **Bewertung:** For a Phase-1 offline-first SQLite app this is, honestly, a reasonable and above-average scaffold for a bachelor project. The local-first write path is correct, the Strategy abstraction is clean and lets each entity define its own sync semantics, the session conflict logic is thoughtful, client-side UUIDs set up idempotency well, and the Dio/auth layer is competently written. ### Navigation & Routing

**Ist-Zustand:** The app uses three uncoordinated navigation mechanisms layered on top of each other:

1. Navigator 1.0 static named routes. `lib/main.dart` (lines 224-232) declares 7 routes in a `MaterialApp.routes` map (`/`, `/login`, `/register`, `/verify`, `/forgot-password`, `/reset-password`, `/home`) with `onUnknownRoute` falling back to SplashScreen. Auth/screen transitions use `Navigator.pushReplacementNamed`/`pushNamed`/`pushNamedAndRemoveUntil` scattered across screens (e.g. `splash_screen.dart:44`, `login_screen.dart:100`, `profile_screen.dart:529`).

2. Custom IndexedStack tabs. `lib/presentation/navigation/main_navigation.dart` is a single StatefulWidget holding `int _currentIndex` and a `const List<Widget> _screens` of 5 tab roots, swapped via `setState`. Tabs are NOT routes — they have no URL, no Navigator entries, and no per-tab navigation stack. There are no nested/child Navigators (grep for `Navigator(` and per-tab `GlobalKey<NavigatorState>` found none).

3. Imperative `Navigator.push(MaterialPageRoute(...))` for detail screens that are never registered as routes — DeviceConnectionScreen (`activity_screen.dart:261`, `profile_screen.dart:384`), BenefitQrScreen (`benefit_screen.dart:212`), SessionDetailScreen (`progress_screen.dart:306`). These pass typed data via constructor args (good type safety) but are consequently unreachable by deep link or URL.

Cross-cutting: a single global `navigatorKey` (`main.dart:29`) is used by the deep-link handler and by `_handlePasswordRequired` (`main.dart:194`) to force-navigate from outside the widget tree. Route guarding is entirely ad-hoc and imperative: the SplashScreen (`splash_screen.dart:23-47`) is the ONLY auth gate — it calls `userProvider.initialize()` then `pushReplacementNamed('/home' or '/login')`. There is no declarative redirect; nothing prevents `pushNamed('/home')` while unauthenticated. The app-lock is an overlay in `MaterialApp.builder` (`main.dart:213-220`), not a route.

Deep links: `lib/core/deep_link/deep_link_handler.dart` uses `app_links` (`pubspec.yaml:97`, pinned to `any`). It handles exactly ONE link — custom scheme `benefit://reset-password?token=xxx` — via a manual `switch(uri.host)` and pushes to `/reset-password` with `arguments: {'token': token}`. `reset_password_screen.dart:56` reads it back via `ModalRoute.of(context)?.settings.arguments` with an `is Map<String,String>` cast (unsafe, untyped). Android manifest (`AndroidManifest.xml:64-69`) registers the custom-scheme intent-filter (no `autoVerify`/App Links, no https); iOS Info.plist registers the `benefit` CFBundleURLScheme. A `web/` directory exists (index.html, manifest.json) so web is a build target, but there is no `go_router`/`url_strategy`/`flutter_web_plugins` usage — URLs are not real navigation and the custom `benefit://` scheme does not work on web.

Testability: `test/helpers/pump_app.dart` wraps widgets in a bare `MaterialApp(home: child)` with no routes. `test/integration/` is empty and there are 0 `testWidgets` occurrences in the suite — navigation flows, the auth gate, and deep-link handling have zero automated coverage.


**Bewertung:** For a Phase-1, offline-first, mostly-linear app this navigation works and is easy to read — that is a genuine strength and the simplicity should be respected. Navigator 1.0 named routes are a legitimate, officially-supported Flutter pattern; "switch to go_router" is not automatically correct. Two things are actually done well: detail screens (SessionDetailScreen, BenefitQrScreen, DeviceConnectionScreen) pass data via typed constructors rather than untyped route arguments, which is the type-safe choice; and the IndexedStack tab approach correctly preserves each tab's state across switches, which is the right call for a 5-tab fitness app.

However, measured against the stated goals (large-scale public rollout + maximally modern/modular), there are real structural weaknesses, not cosmetic ones:

- The auth guard is a single imperative checkpoint in SplashScreen. Any future `pushNamed('/home')` or a deep link that lands on an authenticated screen has NO guard — security/correctness depends on every call site behaving. This does not scale as the route count grows.
- Deep linking is effectively a one-off. It handles a single custom-scheme host with a hand-written switch and an unsafe argument cast. Adding more deep links means more bespoke switch/cast code, and the most valuable deep targets (a specific benefit, a session) aren't routable at all because they're constructor-arg pushes. Custom scheme `benefit://` is also weak for production (no App Links/Universal Links = no https links from emails/SMS, hijackable scheme); for a public reset-password flow this is a real robustness gap.
- Web is a declared target but URLs are meaningless (no path-based routing, no browser back/forward semantics, deep links don't work). If web matters, the current setup does not deliver it.
- Navigation is untested (0 widget tests, empty integration dir). The auth gate and the only deep link have no regression protection.
- Mixing three navigation styles (named routes + IndexedStack + anonymous MaterialPageRoute) plus a global navigatorKey escape hatch is exactly the kind of "no enforced boundary" inconsistency that gets harder to reason about as the team/feature count grows.

The honest verdict: the current approach is FINE for today's scope but is a poor foundation for the stated rollout ambitions. The single highest-leverage change is consolidating onto a declarative, guarded router (go_router) — it directly fixes the guard, deep-link, and web gaps at once. auto_route buys more compile-time type safety but adds codegen the project currently avoids (no build_runner), so it's a worse fit here.

### Auth & Security (for large public rollout)

**Ist-Zustand:** Phase 1 is offline-first/SQLite-only and the auth stack is deliberately mock-driven, but the security scaffolding is unusually mature for a bachelor project. The live app wires `SecureTokenStorage()` + `MockAuthService()` in main.dart:55-56. `AuthService` (lib/features/auth/data/auth_service.dart) is a clean interface with only `MockAuthService` implemented; the real backend (`RealAuthService`) does not exist yet. Tokens are modeled by `AuthTokens` (accessToken/refreshToken/expiresAt) with `isExpired`/`needsRefresh` (5-min skew) getters and persisted via flutter_secure_storage with `encryptedSharedPreferences:true` (Android) and `KeychainAccessibility.first_unlock_this_device` (iOS) in token_storage.dart, rate_limit_storage.dart, and security_preferences.dart. Password hashing is bare SHA-256 (no salt, no KDF) in lib/core/utils/password_utils.dart; this same SHA-256 hash is also stored in the local SQLite `User.passwordHash` (user.dart, user_dao.dart, seed_data.dart) and is verified entirely client-side in profile_screen.dart:1069 for the change-password flow. ### Performance, Scalability & Device Resource Use

**Ist-Zustand:** Offline-first Flutter app on raw sqflite with hand-written DAOs. Activity history is loaded in full into memory: ProgressProvider.loadActivities() calls SessionRepositoryImpl.getAllSessions(userId) (lib/providers/progress_provider.dart:141, lib/features/session/data/session_repository_impl.dart:43) which maps EVERY session row, then ActivitiesTab renders them with a plain ListView(children: [...]) that eagerly builds all rows (lib/presentation/screens/progress/widgets/activities_tab.dart:93). All statistics (per-weekday/month/year distance & duration) are computed client-side by iterating the full in-memory _combinedActivities list on every chart build (lib/providers/progress_provider.dart:244-358, called from statistics_tab.dart:175); there is no SQL SUM/GROUP BY for aggregates. DB indexing is actually decent: 25 CREATE INDEX statements incl. composite idx_gps_points_session_timestamp and idx_sessions_user_id/start_time (lib/features/shared/database/database_helper.dart). GPS is captured via geolocator getPositionStream with LocationAccuracy.high + distanceFilter:5 (lib/features/shared/sensors/gps_sensor.dart:153), with a thoughtful config layer distinguishing manual vs continuous mode (lib/core/config/gps_tracking_config.dart). Each accepted GPS point is written with a single-row _gpsPointDao.insert() and a debugPrint per emission (lib/providers/activity_provider.dart:_onGpsPoint ~660-678); a batched insertBatch() exists in gps_point_dao.dart:59 but is NOT used for live tracking. Sensor/timer subscriptions are correctly cancelled in dispose() (activity_provider.dart:859). Charts are hand-rolled with CustomPaint/Stack (custom_charts.dart) — no chart dependency. Background execution: AndroidManifest declares ACCESS_BACKGROUND_LOCATION + ACTIVITY_RECOGNITION and iOS Info.plist has NSLocationAlwaysAndWhenInUse, but there is NO Android foreground service, NO UIBackgroundModes:location, and NO flutter_background_service/workmanager — getPositionStream runs with default settings (no foregroundNotificationConfig). The session route map renders ALL GPS points as one Polyline with no simplification (session_detail_screen.dart:159-188). Assets total 77MB, dominated by assets/images/runners/ (73MB: run_3.png 36MB, run_2.png 30MB, run_4.png 7MB) loaded full-resolution via AssetImage/Image.asset into a 180px banner and small cards with no cacheWidth/cacheHeight (community_screen.dart:27-69,129,232,347). Profile avatar uses image_picker.pickImage with no maxWidth/imageQuality (profile_screen.dart:549). minSdk 26.


**Bewertung:** For a Phase-1 offline prototype this is in better shape than the dependency situation suggests, and credit is due where earned: the DB schema is sensibly indexed (composite session+timestamp index on gps_points is exactly right), the GPS config layer is genuinely well thought out (separate manual vs continuous thresholds, quality filtering, retention policy), subscriptions and timers are disposed correctly, and the hand-rolled charts — while verbose — render fixed small datasets (7/12/6 buckets) so they are NOT a performance problem and should not be 'modernized' for perf reasons. That said, three issues are real and disqualifying for a large-scale rollout. (1) The 73MB of runner PNGs is the single worst problem: it bloats the install/download size enormously and, because they are decoded at full 36MP resolution into tiny widgets with no cacheWidth, each one inflates to hundreds of MB in the image cache and will OOM-crash low-end devices the moment the Community tab opens. This is critical and cheap to fix. (2) The 'load everything into memory + aggregate in Dart' pattern is fine for the seeded demo but does not scale: a daily-continuous-tracking user produces a session per day plus thousands of GPS points; getAllSessions + full-list re-aggregation on every rebuild becomes a jank/memory liability within months of real use, and ListView(children:) (not ListView.builder) means every row is built up front. (3) Background tracking is effectively non-functional for the stated continuous-movement use case: the permissions are declared but the plumbing (foreground service / iOS background modes) is absent, so Android will kill location updates seconds after backgrounding. The continuous-mode config exists but has no runtime to run in. The good news: none of these require the trendy libraries flagged as 'absent' — they're concrete, scoped fixes.


**Skalierungs-Risiken:**
